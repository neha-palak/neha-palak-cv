SIFT Keypoints & Features

  • SIFT detects distinctive keypoints and computes feature descriptors for each image
  • Keypoints are concentrated on edges and textured regions
  • These features are used for matching across images

Challenges & Improvements

Initially, SIFT detected a very large number of keypoints (in the range of 10,000–20,000 per image), many of which were from noisy or less informative regions.

To improve matching quality and reduce noise, we filtered the keypoints by selecting only the top 500 based on their response values. This resulted in more stable and meaningful feature matching.

Feature Matching

  • Features are matched using Brute Force Matcher
  • KNN matching with ratio test removes weak matches
  • Matching cost is defined as average distance of good matches

Observations

  • Overall: Most consecutive pairs have similar costs → due to smooth camera movement and high overlap
  • Very Low Cost: (0013–0015) has very low cost → images are almost identical → strong matches
  • Higher Cost: Some pairs show higher cost → due to viewpoint change and reduced overlap
  • Highest Cost: (0037–0001) has highest cost → start and end images are very different
  • Key Insight: Lower cost = more similar images, Higher cost = less similarity
