Problem 1:

In the first image, the photograph is taken from very close to the subject. Because the camera is near the face, parts of the face closer to the camera (such as the nose and mouth) appear disproportionately larger than parts farther away (such as the ears). This results in noticeable perspective distortion and an unnatural appearance.

In the second image, the camera is placed farther away and zoomed in so that the face appears approximately the same size as in the first image. Increasing the camera-to-subject distance reduces the relative depth differences across facial features, thereby reducing perspective distortion. As a result, the facial proportions appear more natural and visually pleasing.

This demonstrates that perspective distortion depends primarily on camera distance rather than zoom or focal length.
